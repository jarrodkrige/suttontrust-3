Resources We Trust
These are well‑known, reliable UK websites that students (and schools) regularly use for accurate, up‑to‑date information about university, finance, and student life.

UCAS – The official hub for applying to UK universities, with clear guidance on courses, applications, and student finance.
Student Finance England (GOV.UK) – The official government site for tuition fee loans, maintenance loans, grants, repayment rules, and eligibility.
Save the Student – A trusted student‑led site offering practical advice on budgeting, part‑time work, bank accounts, and saving money at uni.
MoneySavingExpert – Students – Independent guidance on student loans, overdrafts, budgeting tools, and financial myths.
The Complete University Guide – Independent UK university rankings, subject guides, and student advice.

Why this matters: when you’re the first in your family to go to university, knowing which sources are trustworthy can save you time, stress, and money.




Voices: Their Journeys Started at UK Universities
Short quotes from well‑known people who studied at UK universities — showing that uncertain beginnings can still lead to extraordinary outcomes.

Stephen Hawking – University of Cambridge
“Intelligence is the ability to adapt to change.”
Hawking studied mathematics and physics at Cambridge and went on to become one of the most influential scientists in modern history.





David Attenborough – University of Cambridge
“An understanding of the natural world is a source of not only great curiosity, but great fulfilment.”
Attenborough’s Cambridge degree laid the foundation for a lifetime of science communication and storytelling that inspired generations.





Zadie Smith – University of Cambridge
“I was lucky to stumble into an environment that encouraged thought.”
As a student at Cambridge, Smith began writing seriously, later becoming one of the UK’s most celebrated authors.





Tim Berners‑Lee – University of Oxford
“The Web is more a social creation than a technical one.”
Berners‑Lee studied physics at Oxford before inventing the World Wide Web — reshaping how the world shares information.





Alan Turing – University of Cambridge (later University of Manchester)
“Those who can imagine anything, can create the impossible.”
Turing’s academic journey through UK universities underpinned work that defined computing and helped end World War II.





Takeaway: None of these journeys were obvious at the start. University didn’t give them certainty — it gave them space to grow.
