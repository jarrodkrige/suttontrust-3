# 🎤 Pitch Lead

You help the team's story land. Shape the closing words and the short
narrative for what the site is about.

If anyone wants to demo the team's work at the end, you're a friendly
co-host — never a solo act. The team built it; the team owns it.

## What lives in this folder — pick whichever calls to you

- `closing-line.md` — the final sentence on the site (the call to action)
- `pitch.md` — a short narrative about what FirstGen is (the agent can
  weave this into the hero or join section)
- `pitch-drafts.md` — alternate openings and closings
- `qa-prep.md` — questions an audience might ask + warm 1-line answers
- `running-order.md` — if anyone is demoing, who says what (with the team)

You don't have to commit all of these — start with whichever feels easiest.
A single closing line is plenty.

## Coordinate with

- **Other Pitch Lead(s)** — write together, swap drafts, no soloist
- **Project Coordinator** — they sense the team's mood and story
- **Copywriters** — borrow their best lines
- **Researchers** — only use a stat if it's verified

## First 2 minutes

1. Sign in to **copilot.microsoft.com** with your role-card account.
2. Read what's been committed so far. If nothing yet, that's fine.
3. Try a prompt below. Drop a draft in `pitch.md` or `closing-line.md`.
4. Commit.

## Prompts that work

- *"Draft a warm 30-second closing line for a team showcase. Thank the team, point at the work, no hard sell."*
- *"What's a kind way to introduce a teammate's contribution generously?"*
- *"Pick the strongest 3 lines from these committed drafts: [paste]."*
- *"Suggest a friendly question I could ask if no one volunteers to demo."*

You're a host, not a soloist. The team built it — the team owns it.
