A. Mental health & wellbeing
Stat 1
57% of UK university students self‑report a mental health issue; 27% report a diagnosed condition [commonslib...liament.uk]
Source: Student Minds survey, cited by the UK House of Commons Library
Bias / limitations:

Self‑reported → likely over‑represents students already struggling
Mental health charities naturally focus on harm prevalence
Not all students disclose mental health concerns to surveys

Why relevant to sixth‑formers:
Counters the assumption that “only a small group struggle at uni”. It sets realistic expectations about support needs.

Stat 2
37% of first‑year students experience moderate to severe anxiety or depression, nearly double non‑students aged 16–29 [mentalheal...urse.co.uk]
Source: Office for National Statistics (ONS) data summarised in mental‑health analysis
Bias / limitations:

ONS uses validated mental‑health screening, not clinical diagnosis
First‑year heavy weighting → transition stress may inflate figures

Why relevant:
Sixth‑formers are about to enter first year — this stat applies directly to them, not graduates or final‑year students.

Stat 3
Nearly three‑quarters of students report loneliness; 17% say they have no university friends [mentalheal...urse.co.uk]
Source: ONS + Student Academic Experience data
Bias / limitations:

Loneliness is subjective
Post‑pandemic cohorts may skew higher

Why relevant:
Challenges the “constant social life” myth seen in marketing materials.

B. Money & work
Stat 4
68% of full‑time undergraduates work during term‑time (up from 42% in 2020) [hepi.ac.uk]
Source: Student Academic Experience Survey (SAES), HEPI & Advance HE
Bias / limitations:

Focuses on full‑time undergraduates only
Cost‑of‑living period inflates employment rates compared to earlier decades

Why relevant:
Shows university is rarely “just studying” anymore — critical for sixth‑form budgeting realism.

Stat 5
Average independent study time fell to 11.6 hours per week [hepi.ac.uk]
Source: SAES 2025
Bias / limitations:

Self‑reported hours
Doesn’t measure study quality

Why relevant:
Helps prospective students understand workload pressure and time management demands.

Stat 6
51% of students say cost‑of‑living negatively affects mental health [moneyandme...health.org]
Source: Student Minds / Money and Mental Health
Bias / limitations:

Charity‑commissioned survey → problem‑focused framing
Done during a financial crisis peak

Why relevant:
Money stress is a predictable factor incoming students can prepare for.

C. Dropping out & regret
Stat 7
41,914 UK students dropped out in 2022–23 (highest on record) [timeshighe...cation.com]
Source: Student Loans Company via Times Higher Education
Bias / limitations:

Only includes students receiving loans
Does not track students who transfer successfully

Why relevant:
Dispels the idea that leaving university is rare or catastrophic.

Stat 8
11% of students would not choose higher education again if deciding privately [hepi.ac.uk]
Source: SAES 2025
Bias / limitations:

Hypothetical hindsight question
Does not mean regret = failure

Why relevant:
Encourages informed choice rather than automatic progression.

D. Employment outcomes
Stat 9
88% of graduates are in work or further study 15 months after graduating [graduateou...omes.ac.uk]
Source: HESA Graduate Outcomes Survey
Bias / limitations:

Surveyed graduates who respond may be doing better
Counts any work, not just graduate‑level roles

Why relevant:
Balances fear‑based dropout narratives — university still offers long‑term benefits.

Stat 10
Graduates earn ~33% more than non‑graduates by age 31 [universitiesuk.ac.uk]
Source: Universities UK analysis of government LEO data
Bias / limitations:

Averages hide subject and institution differences
Not a guarantee for every individual

Why relevant:
Helps students think long‑term rather than focusing only on student debt.
