Misconception:

“First‑generation (first‑in‑family) university students are a small, clearly disadvantaged minority.”

Why this is misleading (what the charity could explain instead):
Research shows that first‑in‑family students actually make up a majority of UK undergraduates under common definitions, and their backgrounds and levels of disadvantage vary widely. Some face significant financial and cultural barriers, but others have strong social, financial, or educational support. As a result, first‑generation status alone is not a reliable proxy for disadvantage and works best when used alongside other indicators (such as household income or free school meal eligibility).
This misconception matters because it can lead to oversimplified narratives, poorly targeted support, and the assumption that all first‑generation students have the same needs.
Evidence to cite (with research link):

The Higher Education Policy Institute (HEPI) found that just over two‑thirds of UK students can be classified as “first‑in‑family” under some definitions, and warned that the category is too broad and inconsistently defined to be used on its own for high‑stakes decisions.  https://www.hepi.ac.uk/2022/01/06/new-report-finds-first-in-family-status-flawed-as-a-way-of-helping-disadvantaged-students/
