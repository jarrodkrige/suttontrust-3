# 🔎 Researcher

You find real-world evidence and context for the team — statistics, real
student quotes, sources. You bring the trust-layer the rest of the team's
words rest on.

Multiple researchers in the team — you all contribute together. Split the
work however feels right (stats vs quotes is a common split).

## What lives in this folder — pick whichever calls to you

- `stats.md` — statistics about first-generation UK university students,
  with sources
- `quotes.md` — real first-person quotes (verified — never invented)
- `personas.md` — short profiles of typical first-gen sixth-formers (to
  inform copy choices)
- `similar-initiatives.md` — UK charities and programmes already in this
  space
- `cross-checks.md` — where you've double-checked someone else's stat
- `sources.md` — every URL you used so the Product Owner can verify

**Claim a lane with your teammates first** — stats, quotes, personas,
similar initiatives.

## Coordinate with

- **Other Researcher(s)** — agree the split early
- **Copywriters** — they consume your evidence
- **Pitch Lead(s)** — they may use a stat in the opening line
- **Product Owner (Jarrod)** — they validate whether a source is solid

## First 2 minutes

1. Sign in to **copilot.microsoft.com** with your role-card account.
2. Try a prompt below.
3. Verify the most useful result by clicking the source link.
4. Drop it in `stats.md` or `quotes.md` with the URL.

## Prompts that work

- *"Give me 5 statistics about first-generation university students in the UK. For each, tell me the source and the year. If you're not sure of the source, say so."*
- *"Find 3 first-person quotes from first-gen UK uni students. Cite the source. If you can't find real quotes, say so — don't invent them."*
- *"Where does the claim '[paste claim]' come from? Check the original source, not just where it's repeated."*
- *"Cross-check this stat — '[paste]' — does the original source say what we're claiming?"*

The AI will make stuff up — especially quotes. Always verify with a click
before committing. When in doubt, ask the AI to tell you it doesn't know.
