## 1) How long do a degree, subject, and university matter?

### Early career (0–5 years after graduation)

**What matters most**

*   **Subject alignment and vocational exposure** strongly shape early outcomes.
*   LEO data (tax‑linked) shows large **subject‑level gaps** in earnings and employment five years out (e.g., economics, engineering, computing outperform creative arts). [\[explore-ed...ice.gov.uk\]](https://explore-education-statistics.service.gov.uk/find-statistics/leo-graduate-and-postgraduate-outcomes/2022-23), [\[savethestudent.org\]](https://www.savethestudent.org/graduates/this-is-how-much-your-degree-will-earn-you-in-10-years.html)
*   Employers value **relevant skills and work experience during the degree** more than institution name; only \~8% cite university attended as very important at hire, versus skills and subject relevance. [\[unialliance.ac.uk\]](https://www.unialliance.ac.uk/2024/08/14/towhatdegree/)

**Bias / limits**

*   Early snapshots **overweight linear pipelines** (internships → job).
*   Graduate Outcomes (15 months) favours routes that convert quickly; it **understates slow‑build careers** (research, creative, portfolio work).

***

### Mid‑career (5–15 years out)

**What changes**

*   The **degree acts as a baseline credential**; **experience dominates**.
*   Earnings growth for graduates **outpaces non‑graduates** between ages \~23–31, but the slope varies by sector and role changes. [\[universitiesuk.ac.uk\]](https://www.universitiesuk.ac.uk/latest/insights-and-analysis/graduate-outcomes-what-latest-data)
*   Employer surveys increasingly show **skills‑based hiring**, with a declining share of postings explicitly requiring degrees (only \~14% mention education requirements; \~5% require a degree). [\[hrreview.co.uk\]](https://hrreview.co.uk/hr-news/recruitment/only-14-of-job-postings-mention-education-requirements-while-only-5-need-a-degree/376473)

**What still matters**

*   **Regulated professions** (medicine, engineering, dentistry, research) continue to require specific degrees/licensing; degree relevance persists. [\[hrreview.co.uk\]](https://hrreview.co.uk/hr-news/recruitment/only-14-of-job-postings-mention-education-requirements-while-only-5-need-a-degree/376473)

**Bias / limits**

*   LEO misses **self‑employment and overseas work**; it is conservative for entrepreneurial paths. [\[gov.uk\]](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/longitudinal-education-outcomes-leo-dataset/longitudinal-education-outcomes-leo-data)

***

### Late career (15+ years)

**What matters least**

*   **University brand** and precise subject generally fade for non‑regulated roles.
*   Hiring decisions revolve around **track record, leadership, and domain credibility**.

**What can still matter**

*   **Professional closures** (chartered status, licenses) and academic research careers where credentials are durable.
