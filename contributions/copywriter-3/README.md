# ✍️ Copywriter

You write the words on the site. Warm, clear, trustworthy — for 16–18
year-olds wondering if uni is really for "people like them".

Multiple copywriters in the team — you all contribute together. The agent
picks the strongest version per section and weaves in other useful drafts
where they strengthen the page.

## What lives in this folder — pick whichever calls to you

- `headlines.md` — hero headline + alternates
- `hero-copy.md` — the lede paragraph under the headline
- `cta.md` — call-to-action variants
- `body-copy.md` — text for content sections (what / who / how)
- `microcopy.md` — small bits: nav labels, button text, footer line
- `voice-notes.md` — tone rules you'd defend (reading age, contractions,
  things to avoid)
- `alternative-drafts.md` — re-takes on any section, where you spot a
  sharper version

**Claim a lane with your teammates first** — hero, body, microcopy,
voice/polish, alternatives. The team covers more ground that way.

## Coordinate with

- **Other Copywriters** — read what they've dropped before you write; agree
  the team's voice in the first few minutes
- **Researchers** — they surface real stats and quotes you can weave in
- **Pitch Lead(s)** — they shape the closing line; share your strongest
  drafts with them
- **Accessibility Champion(s)** — they review for plain language

## First 2 minutes

1. Sign in to **copilot.microsoft.com** with your role-card account.
2. Pick a lane (headline / body / microcopy / voice / alternatives).
3. Try a prompt below. Drop the best version into a file in your folder.
4. Commit.

## Prompts that work

- *"I'm writing copy for a charity microsite called FirstGen. Audience: 16–18 year-olds in the UK who are first in their family to consider university. Give me 5 hero headline options that feel warm, honest and inviting. Avoid clichés."*
- *"Rewrite this headline for a more anxious reader."* / *"... for a more excited reader."*
- *"Shorten this paragraph to 25 words without losing the warmth."*
- *"What's the reading age of this passage? Aim for 14."*
- *"Take this paragraph and make it sound like a friend talking to a friend: [paste]."*
