> Team 3 · Role accessibility-champion-1 · 2026-05-08 13:54

1. Structure & Navigation

Use proper HTML landmarks (header, main, nav, footer)
Ensure there is one clear <h1> for the page (usually the hero headline)
Use headings in logical order (h2, h3) without skipping levels
Navigation and CTA links are usable with keyboard only
Anchor links move focus to the target section (not just scroll visually)


2. Content & Text

Use plain, readable language (avoid jargon where possible)
Keep paragraphs short and scannable
Do not rely on colour alone to convey meaning (e.g. “required”, “error”)
Link text makes sense on its own (avoid “click here”)


3. Images

All meaningful images have clear, brief alt text
Decorative images have empty alt (alt="")
Avoid embedding important text inside images
Logos link back to the homepage and have alt text


4. Colour & Contrast

Text has strong contrast against backgrounds (especially body text and CTAs)
Links are visually distinguishable from surrounding text
Focus states (hover / focus) are clearly visible
Avoid very light text on images or gradients


5. Forms (Contact / Donation)

Every input has a visible label
Required fields are indicated in text, not just colour
Errors are described in text and linked to the relevant field
Form can be completed using keyboard only
The submit button clearly describes its action (e.g. “Donate now”)


6. Interaction & Focus

All interactive elements are reachable by keyboard (Tab / Shift+Tab)
Focus order follows the visual layout
No keyboard traps (users can move in and out of sections)
Buttons are real buttons (<button>), not plain divs or spans


7. General

Page works when zoomed to 200% without breaking layout
No auto‑playing audio or video
Page has a descriptive <title>
